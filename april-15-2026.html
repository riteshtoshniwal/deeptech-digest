🤖 AI & Machine Learning

India Leads APAC in Agentic AI Adoption

India has emerged as the Asia-Pacific frontrunner in agentic AI adoption, with the highest proportion of expert users in the region. According to a new report, India leads globally in realizing measurable returns from agentic AI applications — 50% of respondents reported productivity gains from code generation and workflow automation, outpacing all other markets.

  • IANS Live — India Leads APAC in Agentic AI Adoption

Enterprise AI Deployment Reaches 40% in India

Deloitte's 2026 State of AI in the Enterprise report reveals that 40% of Indian enterprises have reached significant or full AI deployment — compared to just 28% globally. However, deep technical AI expertise still lags, with only 0–4% of Indian organizations reporting mature AI skillsets, pointing to a talent gap that needs urgent attention.

  • Asanify — India Enterprise AI Adoption, April 2026

New Hyperscale Data Centre in Hyderabad Goes Live Mid-2026

A new hyperscale data centre in Hyderabad is set to go live mid-2026 with three availability zones, significantly boosting India's AI infrastructure capacity. Microsoft has simultaneously doubled its commitment to train 20 million Indians in AI skills by 2030, reinforcing the country's growing AI talent and infrastructure ecosystem.

  • Asanify — AI Infrastructure India Investment, April 2026

  • TechCrunch — Indian Startup Rocket AI Consulting Platform

Total capital deployed across Indian startups in Q1 2026 reached ₹18,240 crore — a 23% increase over Q4 2025. Analysts note a shift toward fewer but larger, more strategic investments.

In 2025, deep tech companies raised $562 million across 53 equity rounds — a 4% year-on-year rise — signalling sustained institutional interest in the region's innovation stack.

Singapore's startup ecosystem captured $5.4 billion of total Southeast Asian startup capital raised in 2025 — representing 91% of the regional total. 🔐 Cybersecurity

Cybersecurity Ranked India's #1 National Risk

The World Economic Forum's Global Risk Report 2026 has ranked cybersecurity as India's top national risk — ahead of economic downturns, climate disasters, and armed conflict. Nearly a quarter of Indian enterprises have reported cyber breach losses exceeding $1 million in the past three years, underscoring the severity and frequency of attacks targeting the country's digital infrastructure.

  • Cyble — India Cyber Threat Landscape 2026

SEA Gaming Cyberattacks Surge 86% in H2 2025

Kaspersky has reported an 86% surge in gaming-related cyberthreats across Southeast Asia in the second half of 2025. Vietnam saw a staggering 202.5% increase, Thailand 104.4%, and Malaysia 21.3%. Attacks are targeting popular platforms including Roblox, Minecraft, and Genshin Impact, exploiting their large, young user bases.

  • Business Today Malaysia — Gaming Cyberthreats SEA

CPUID Supply Chain Breach via Trojanized Downloads

A sophisticated supply chain attack on CPUID — developer of popular hardware monitoring tools CPU-Z and HWMonitor — involved trojanized downloads and infiltration via STX RAT malware. The breach exploited the company's download infrastructure to deliver malware to users, highlighting the growing risk of software supply chain attacks in the developer tools ecosystem.

  • ConnectQuest — CPUID Breach Analysis, April 12 2026

JFrog and iZeno Partner for Secure AI Software Delivery in SEA

JFrog and iZeno announced a strategic partnership on April 7, 2026, to accelerate secure AI software delivery across Southeast Asia. The collaboration combines JFrog's software supply chain security platform with iZeno's regional expertise, aiming to help enterprises in the region adopt AI safely and at scale.

  • Cybersecurity Asia — JFrog iZeno Partnership

📜 Tech Policy & Regulations

India's IT Amendment Rules 2026: AI Content Labelling & 3-Hour Takedowns

India's new IT Amendment Rules 2026 mandate AI-generated content labelling, stricter intermediary obligations, and reduce social media takedown windows to just 3 hours for harmful content. Industry stakeholders have flagged clarity concerns around implementation, though the government has assured flexibility in enforcement as the ecosystem adapts.

  • Outlook Business — IT Rules 2026 Stakeholder Concerns
  • Tech AI Mag — India's 2026 IT Rules Overview

RBI Proposes Trusted-Person Authentication for High-Value Payments

The Reserve Bank of India has proposed a new "trusted-person authentication" layer for high-value digital payments, aimed at curbing fraud targeting elderly and vulnerable users. The proposal is open for public comment until May 8, 2026 and could significantly reshape how India's digital payments authentication stack evolves.

  • Outlook Business — RBI Trusted-Person Authentication

💡 Key Takeaways

India's AI momentum is accelerating on multiple fronts — from enterprise adoption (40% deployment vs 28% globally) to infrastructure investment (new hyperscale data centres) and a booming funding environment (Q1 2026 up 23%). The country's AI-first startup ecosystem is maturing rapidly, with Sarvam AI's $1.5B valuation raising as a bellwether.

Cybersecurity is now a top-tier national concern for India, officially ranked the #1 national risk by the WEF. The 86% gaming cyberattack surge across SEA and the CPUID supply chain breach are reminders that threat actors are finding increasingly creative attack vectors.

Regulation is catching up with reality — India's IT Amendment Rules 2026 represent a significant tightening of the digital governance framework, with AI content labelling and faster takedown mandates shaping the compliance landscape for both startups and platforms.

Southeast Asia continues to attract deep tech capital, with Singapore anchoring the region and Vietnam emerging as the fastest-growing innovation hub. The $9.77B cumulative deep tech funding figure signals that institutional investors are increasingly treating SEA as a serious deeptech destination.
