Cybersecurity

Indian Organizations Hit by Average 3,195 Cyber Attacks Per Week

India's cyber threat landscape has reached alarming proportions, with organizations facing an average of 3,195 attacks per week according to Check Point's 2026 Cyber Security Report. The education sector bears the highest brunt at 7,684 attacks per organization weekly, followed by government bodies (4,912) and business services (3,747). Rapid digitization, geopolitical tensions, and expanding attack surfaces are all contributing to this surge.

- CyberPeace Foundation

State-Backed APT Campaign Targets India's Defence Sector via Sideloaded DLLs

A sophisticated Advanced Persistent Threat (APT) campaign documented by Seqrite is actively targeting India's defence sector and critical infrastructure. The attackers use MSI installers, sideloaded DLLs, and open-source Remote Access Trojans to exploit trust in legitimate software installations, establishing persistent, long-term access aligned with geopolitical objectives. Security researchers warn that the campaign's tradecraft is evolving rapidly.

Tech Policy & Regulation

India's DPDP Act Moves to Enforcement Phase with Stricter AI Data Rules

India's Digital Personal Data Protection (DPDP) Act is entering active enforcement, introducing stricter consent management requirements, Significant Data Fiduciary obligations, and enhanced scrutiny on AI-driven data processing. Full applicability across all entities and government departments is set for May 13, 2027. Organizations processing high volumes of sensitive personal data must now appoint a Data Protection Officer physically based in India.
- Prashant Mali Cyber Law Blog
- IAPP

India's IT Amendment Rules 2026 Mandate AI Content Labeling and 3-Hour Takedowns

Effective February 20, 2026, India's IT Amendment Rules now require mandatory labeling of AI-generated content and compress the window for removing harmful content to just 3 hours. The rules also specify data retention schedules, breach notification timelines, and audit obligations for Significant Data Fiduciaries. Stakeholders have raised clarity concerns, though the government has assured flexibility in implementation.
- Outlook Business
- AI Dev Day India

India's AI Ethics Bill Proposes Statutory Ethics Committee and Bias Audits

An Artificial Intelligence (Ethics and Accountability) Bill, introduced in the Lok Sabha in December 2025, proposes creating a statutory Ethics Committee for AI, mandating ethical reviews for surveillance and high-risk systems, and requiring bias audits with developer obligations. Penalties of up to ₹5 crore are proposed for violations. The framework leverages the Information Technology Act, 2000 as its governance backbone.

Vietnam's landmark Personal Data Protection Law came into force on January 1, 2026, strengthening privacy protections, enhancing supervisory mechanisms, and mandating greater transparency in personal data management. Key Takeaways

India is building foundational AI infrastructure at scale. From the AIKosh dataset platform to the India Deep Tech Alliance's $1B+ war chest and the government's doubled startup classification window, India is systematically de-risking long-horizon deeptech bets.

Cyber threats are escalating faster than defences. With Indian organizations absorbing 3,195 attacks per week and SEA's iOS vulnerability surge of 1,151%, the region faces a widening security gap. AI-powered attack tooling — from deepfakes to APT campaigns — is lowering the barrier to entry for sophisticated threat actors.

The regulatory wave is cresting. From India's DPDP enforcement and IT Amendment Rules to Vietnam's data law and ASEAN's DEFA negotiations, policymakers across the region are rapidly formalising digital governance. Enterprises operating regionally face a patchwork of overlapping obligations.

Singapore is doubling down as the AI hub. With Google's Majulah AI, Microsoft's QuickStart and Go Local programs, and a government-backed plan to triple AI practitioners by 2029, Singapore is executing a coordinated national strategy to anchor Southeast Asia's AI economy.

Investment momentum remains strong. India ($936M raised through April 2026) and Southeast Asia ($9.77B total) are both seeing accelerating deal flow, with AI and deep tech attracting the bulk of capital as the macro environment for tech investment recovers.
