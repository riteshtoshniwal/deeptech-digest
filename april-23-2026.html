🇮🇳 INDIA — FUNDING & INVESTMENTS

KRAFTON, Naver & Mirae Asset Launch ₹6,000 Crore Unicorn Growth Fund for India

KRAFTON Inc., Naver Corporation, and Mirae Asset Venture Investments formally launched the "Unicorn Growth Fund" — a $642 million (₹6,000 crore) investment vehicle — on April 21, 2026 in New Delhi, in the presence of Union Minister Piyush Goyal and South Korea's Trade Minister. The fund will target growth-stage companies across four sectors: technology platforms, consumer businesses, AI and software (including generative AI tools and enterprise developer platforms), and deeptech (spanning semiconductors, robotics, spacetech, and advanced materials). The partners describe it as the largest India-focused capital pool ever raised by an Asian technology-led platform.

India Deep Tech Alliance Commits $1B USD to Indian AI Startups

The India Deep Tech Alliance (IDTA) issued its inaugural report revealing that AI funding in India jumped 58% in 2025, and announced a dedicated $1 billion USD investment commitment to AI startups, to be deployed over the next three years. This forms part of a broader $2.5B USD capital commitment by IDTA members allocated to deep tech investment in India. The report also highlighted that Indian startups collectively raised $9.1 billion in a recent period, with AI driving 91% of deeptech investments.

Startup India FoF 2.0 Expanded to Back Deeptech and Manufacturing

Effective April 13, 2026, the scope of Startup India's Fund of Funds 2.0 has been formally expanded to support deeptech and manufacturing startups, with disbursals to Alternative Investment Funds planned across the 16th and 17th Finance Commission cycles. This signals a significant policy commitment to deeptech scaling beyond software-first startups, creating a funding pipeline for hardware-heavy companies that have historically struggled to attract early capital.
- StartupNews.fyi

Pranos Fusion Closes $6.8M Pre-Series A in Fusion Energy

Bengaluru-based Pranos Fusion closed a $6.8 million pre-Series A round in Q1 2026, co-led by pi Ventures and Ankur Capital. The company is working on fusion energy technology — one of the most ambitious and capital-intensive areas of deeptech — signaling growing investor appetite for hard science bets in India's startup ecosystem.
- LAFFAZ

🇮🇳 INDIA — POLICY & REGULATION

India Forms High-Level AI Governance Body — AIGEG — Headed by Ashwini Vaishnaw

On April 16, 2026, the Indian government constituted the AI Governance and Economic Group (AIGEG), a high-level inter-ministerial body chaired by Union IT Minister Ashwini Vaishnaw, with MoS Jitin Prasada as vice-chairperson. AIGEG will coordinate AI policy across ministries and sectoral regulators, assess labour market impacts of AI adoption, and develop mitigation strategies. Members include the Principal Scientific Adviser, Chief Economic Adviser, NITI Aayog CEO, and secretaries from MeitY, Telecom, Economic Affairs, and Science & Technology departments.

India's Draft IT Rules 2026: AI Labels, 3-Hour Deepfake Takedowns, and New Oversight

India's draft IT Amendment Rules 2026, open for public consultation, introduce mandatory AI content labeling, a 3-hour takedown window for "Synthetically Generated Information" (SGI) flagged as unlawful (including deepfakes), and expanded government blocking powers. Global platforms face significant compliance obligations under the new rules. India does not regulate AI as a standalone technology — instead it governs AI outputs when hosted or transmitted via digital intermediaries that violate Indian law.

India Doubles Startup Classification Period for Deeptech to 20 Years

Under changes to India's startup recognition framework (reported in February 2026 but taking effect this year), deeptech companies are now treated as startups for up to 20 years — double the previous 10-year window — and the revenue threshold for startup-specific tax, grant, and regulatory benefits has been raised to ₹3 billion from ₹1 billion. This significantly expands the pool of deeptech firms eligible for government support programs.
- TechCrunch

🇮🇳 INDIA — CYBERSECURITY

India Faces 3,195 Cyberattacks Per Week Per Organisation; Education, Govt Top Targets

According to the Check Point 2026 Cyber Security Report, Indian organisations face an average of 3,195 cyberattacks per week — with education institutions absorbing the highest volume at 7,684 attacks per organisation per week, followed by government bodies (4,912) and business services (3,747). India faced over 265 million cyberattacks in 2025 overall. AI-driven attacks are now compressing the entire attack cycle from reconnaissance to exploitation into minutes, creating a new class of autonomous threats.

APT Campaign Targets India's Defence Sector Using MSI Installers and Open-Source RATs

Seqrite's India Cyber Threat Report 2026 documents a rapidly evolving Advanced Persistent Threat (APT) campaign specifically targeting India's defence sector and critical infrastructure. Attackers are using MSI installers, sideloaded DLLs, and open-source Remote Access Trojans (RATs) to exploit trust in legitimate software installation processes. Legacy Operational Technology (OT) systems connected to the internet without adequate network segmentation remain a systemic vulnerability, with a single breach capable of cascading into nationwide service disruptions.

Asia-Pacific Tech Spending to Grow 9.3% in 2026, Led by Vietnam (15.4%) and Philippines (12.3%)

Forrester's latest forecast (April 2026) projects 9.3% overall tech spending growth across Asia-Pacific in 2026. Southeast Asia sees varied growth: Vietnam leads at 15.4%, followed by Philippines at 12.3%, Malaysia at 9.5%, Thailand at 6.8%, and Indonesia at 5.0%. Rising AI infrastructure costs and evolving regulations are noted as headwinds that may compress real growth below headline figures.

🔑 KEY TAKEAWAYS

1. India is Scaling Its Deeptech Bet — Institutionally and Financially. The AIGEG formation, FoF 2.0 expansion, 20-year startup status for deeptech, and the KRAFTON/Naver Unicorn Fund all point to a coordinated push to make India a serious deeptech power — not just a software services hub.

2. AI Governance Is Becoming a Competitive Differentiator. India's 3-hour deepfake takedown rule and AIGEG's cross-ministry coordination set India apart from Southeast Asia, where governance frameworks lag far behind AI adoption rates. Companies operating across both regions face increasingly divergent compliance environments.

3. Cybersecurity Risk Is Structural, Not Episodic. India's defence and critical infrastructure sectors face sophisticated APT campaigns, while the sheer volume of attacks (3,195/week/organisation) means that breach prevention is giving way to breach resilience as the primary strategic posture.

4. Southeast Asia's AI Wave Is Real — But Uneven. The iFLYTEK, Grab, and GITEX data collectively confirm that AI adoption in SEA is accelerating rapidly. However, the governance gap and uneven tech spending growth (Vietnam 15.4% vs Indonesia 5%) suggest fragmentation rather than a unified market.

5. Post-Quantum Cryptography Is Entering the Mainstream. The unveiling of Southeast Asia's first PQC patents at GITEX AI Asia is a significant milestone — quantum-resilient security is moving from academic research to product reality in the region.
