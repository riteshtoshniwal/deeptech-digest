🤖 AI & MACHINE LEARNING

Sarvam AI Closes ~$350M Round at $1.5B Valuation

Sarvam AI, India's homegrown large language model startup, has raised $300–350 million at a valuation of $1.5–1.55 billion. The round is led by Bessemer Venture Partners with participation from Nvidia, Amazon, and Prosperity7 Ventures. Sarvam's 105-billion-parameter model, trained from scratch on Indic languages, outperforms Google Gemini Flash on several benchmarks while being significantly cheaper. It is now available on the government's AIKosh platform.
- Bloomberg – India AI Startup Sarvam Raises Funds at $1.5 Billion Valuation
- Outlook Business – Sarvam AI Nears Mega Fundraise

India Funds 12 Sovereign AI Projects; BharatGen Tops with ₹1,058 Cr Allocation

The Indian government is backing 12 organisations to develop indigenous foundational AI models. BharatGen — the IIT Bombay-led consortium — received the highest allocation of ₹1,058.52 crore. BharatGen's Param2 is a 17-billion-parameter multilingual mixture-of-experts model supporting multiple Indic languages for use in governance, education, healthcare, and agriculture. Sarvam AI's 30B and 105B parameter models were unveiled at the India AI Impact Summit 2026 in New Delhi.
- MediaNama – Govt funds 12 AI orgs to develop indigenous models
- Business Standard – India unveils 3 sovereign AI models at Delhi Summit
- ETV Bharat – Sarvam AI and BharatGen at India AI Impact Summit

AI-First Firms in Southeast Asia Face Surging Cyber Risk

A new study reveals that 69% of Southeast Asian respondents said AI tools or models contributed to their most recent cybersecurity incident. Additionally, 67% described AI scraping as a material cost centre, with average annual infrastructure impacts exceeding $372,330. The findings underscore a growing tension between AI-driven productivity gains and the amplified attack surface they create.

Operation TrueChaos: Chinese-Linked APT Targets Southeast Asian Governments

Check Point Research documented Operation TrueChaos in early 2026 — a zero-day exploitation campaign targeting Southeast Asian government networks, attributed to a Chinese-linked threat actor. The campaign is part of a broader pattern identified in the 2025/2026 INTERPOL Asia and South Pacific Cyber Threat Assessment, which also flagged industrial-scale deepfake scams, ransomware attacks, business email compromise (BEC), and widespread infostealer malware.

India's Cyber Threat Landscape 2026: State Actors & Ransomware on the Rise

India ranks cybersecurity as its number one national risk per the World Economic Forum Global Risk 2026 report. Between 2021 and mid-2025, India recorded 2.2 million+ cybersecurity incidents — averaging 3,000+ attacks per day. State-backed threat actors are increasingly targeting financial services, healthcare, telecom, and government platforms. Recent incidents include hospital system hacks in Delhi (June 2025) and a high-profile extortion campaign targeting Star Health Insurance executives.

🚀 DEEPTECH STARTUPS & INVESTMENTS

India's National Quantum Mission Expands Cohort to 17 Startups

The Government of India expanded the National Quantum Mission startup cohort to 17, backing nine new deeptech ventures: Sense-XT, ORVISSEMI, QuBeats, Quantum AI Global, bloq, GDQ Labs, Quantum Biosciences, Bumble Bee Instruments, and SAS Qute Electronics. The mission has already achieved a 1,000 km secure quantum communication milestone — ahead of its 8-year target of 2,000 km. Support is channelled through the RDI framework with TDB and BIRAC as second-level fund managers.
- Business Today – Govt expands National Quantum Mission cohort to 17

Startup India FoF 2.0 Expanded to Include Deeptech and Manufacturing

The Startup India Fund of Funds (FoF) 2.0 has been expanded to cover deeptech and manufacturing sectors, with SIDBI as the implementing agency. Disbursals to Alternative Investment Funds (AIFs) will be spread across the 16th and 17th Finance Commission cycles. In 2026 to date, Indian deeptech companies have raised $936M across 74 equity rounds.

- Tracxn – Deep Tech in India: 2026 Market & Investment Trends

Industrial AI Startup Intellithink Raises ₹17 Cr Seed Round

Pune-based Intellithink, an industrial AI startup focused on machine health and energy efficiency, has closed a ₹17 crore seed round led by Pentathlon Ventures with Anicut Capital and Veltis Capital co-investing. The company's flagship product, Intellivibe, uses AI and proprietary IoT sensors for predictive maintenance, remote monitoring, and fault diagnosis across manufacturing plants.

Vietnam's $2B AI Data Center Project and Rising Southeast Asia Ambitions

A consortium including G42, FPT Corporation, VinaCapital, and Viet Thai Investment Group has proposed a USD 2 billion hyperscale AI data centre in Ho Chi Minh City. Vietnam and the Philippines are being identified as the next frontier for AI data centre expansion in the region, attracting major hyperscalers.

📜 POLICY & REGULATION

Singapore Launches World's First Agentic AI Governance Framework

Singapore has become the first country to release a dedicated governance framework for agentic AI, announced by Minister for Digital Development Josephine Teo at the World Economic Forum. The framework mandates human accountability for AI agents, technical controls, and transparency requirements. Armor convened ASEAN leaders to help enterprises operationalise the new requirements.
- PR Newswire – Singapore Launches World's First Agentic AI Governance Framework

India's DPDP Rules 2025 Begin Reshaping AI Data Governance

The Digital Personal Data Protection (DPDP) Rules 2025, notified in November 2025, are now actively driving compliance planning across India's tech and AI industries. The rules introduce mandatory log retention, purpose-mapping obligations, and a phased timeline culminating in full compliance by May 2027. Firms training AI models face new restrictions on data scraping and undocumented datasets, while the rules are considered part of India's broader sovereign AI stack.
- The Outpost AI – India's New DPDP Rules Transform AI Data Governance
- IAPP – India releases DPDPA rules, AI governance guidelines
- Storyboard18 – AI, DPDP and IT rules in India 2026

India Doubles Startup Recognition Period for Deeptech to 20 Years

India revised its startup definitions under the Startup India programme to extend the recognition period for deeptech companies from 10 to 20 years and raised the revenue threshold for eligibility benefits to ₹3 billion. The change acknowledges the longer development cycles typical of science and engineering-led ventures including quantum, semiconductor, and biotech startups.
- TechCrunch – India has changed its startup rules for deep tech

Indonesia to Sign AI Presidential Regulations in Early 2026

Indonesia is progressing toward a soft AI law or framework, with AI regulations expected to be enacted as Presidential regulations in early 2026, targeting the finance, education, and healthcare sectors. Malaysia's new National AI Office (NAIO), launched in December 2024, is rolling out the AI Technology Plan 2026–2030, charting Malaysia's national AI trajectory.

💡 KEY TAKEAWAYS

1. India's Sovereign AI Stack is Taking Shape: With Sarvam AI's $350M raise and government backing of 12 indigenous AI model projects, India is building a credible alternative to Western and Chinese AI systems — with a specific focus on Indic language capabilities and sovereign governance.

2. Quantum Tech Gets Serious Policy & Capital Backing: India's National Quantum Mission expansion to 17 startups, combined with Andhra Pradesh's quantum valley, signals that India is moving from aspiration to execution in deeptech hardware.

3. Cybersecurity Policy is Lagging AI Adoption Across SEA: Both The Diplomat and regional security researchers note a worrying gap between AI deployment speed and cybersecurity policy readiness in Southeast Asia — a gap that threat actors are actively exploiting.

4. Data Centers are the New Infrastructure Race: Vietnam and the Philippines are emerging as the next major battleground for AI data centre investment in SEA, with multi-billion-dollar commitments from global players including G42, FPT, and hyperscalers.

5. Regulation is Coming — and AI Compliance Will Be Complex: India's DPDP Rules, Singapore's Agentic AI Framework, and Indonesia's forthcoming Presidential AI regulations all indicate that the grace period for unregulated AI deployment in Asia is ending fast.
