India Changes Deep Tech Startup Rules: 20-Year Status & Higher Revenue Cap

🇮🇳 INDIA — CYBERSECURITY

CERT-In Issues Urgent Warning: AI-Powered Cyberattacks Are Escalating

India's Computer Emergency Response Team (CERT-In) issued a high-severity advisory on April 28, 2026, warning that frontier AI models are now enabling cyberattacks at unprecedented scale and speed. AI systems can analyze large codebases for vulnerabilities, conduct automated reconnaissance, and generate highly convincing phishing content — tasks previously requiring skilled security professionals.
- Business Standard — CERT-In Warns of AI-Led Cyber Threats
- MSN / CERT-In — Microsoft Flaws & AI Threat Surge

CERT-In Flags Critical Microsoft Vulnerabilities — Patch Immediately

Alongside its AI threat advisory, CERT-In has urged all Indian organizations to immediately apply Microsoft's April 2026 security updates. The patch cycle addressed 165 vulnerabilities, including 8 critical ones — notably a SharePoint Server spoofing flaw already exploited in the wild, a Windows Kerberos privilege escalation bug, and remote code execution flaws in Remote Desktop Client and Windows TCP/IP.
- MSN — CERT-In Microsoft Flaws Warning

Finance Minister Urges Financial Sector to Fortify Against AI-Driven Cyberattacks

Finance Minister Nirmala Sitharaman, speaking at SEBI's Foundation Day, called on India's financial institutions to proactively strengthen cyber defenses against AI-driven threats. She emphasized investor protection, enhanced regulatory coordination, and international dialogue on AI's potential to destabilize financial markets.

WEF Ranks Cybersecurity as India's #1 National Risk in 2026

The World Economic Forum's Global Risk Report 2026 has ranked cybersecurity as India's top national risk — ahead of economic downturns, climate disasters, and armed conflict. India faced over 265 million cyberattacks in 2025, with AI-driven reconnaissance powering many of them.

🇮🇳 INDIA — AI POLICY & REGULATION

India Forms First Apex AI Governance Body: AIGEG

On April 16, 2026, India formally constituted the AI Governance and Economic Group (AIGEG) — the country's first inter-ministerial apex body dedicated to AI governance. Until this point, India's AI startups operated without a statutory definition of an "AI system," any risk classification framework, or clear liability structures.
- Practice Mock — India AI Policy Panel: Startup Impact (April 27, 2026)
- IAPP — India DPDPA Rules & AI Governance Guidelines

India's Draft IT Rules 2026: AI Content Labels, Deepfake Takedowns, 3-Hour Response Window

India's new IT Amendment Rules 2026 — currently in public consultation — would mandate AI-generated content labeling, impose stricter intermediary obligations, and reduce the harmful content takedown window to just 3 hours. AI-generated content (SGI) provisions were formally notified in February 2026 and are already in effect.

📌 KEY TAKEAWAYS

  1. India's deep tech funding is on a tear. With $936M raised in just the first four months of 2026 and a new policy framework doubling the startup status window, India is laying serious groundwork for a semiconductor, space, and AI-led industrial transition.

  2. AI-powered cyberattacks are India's #1 risk. CERT-In's dual advisory on April 28 — covering both frontier AI threats and critical Microsoft vulnerabilities — signals that the threat landscape is evolving faster than defenses in most organizations.

  3. India is racing to regulate AI. The formation of AIGEG and the draft IT Rules 2026 mark India's shift from regulatory vacuum to structured oversight. Global firms operating in India face increasing compliance obligations.

  4. Southeast Asia is becoming a $78B AI battleground. Between data center mega-investments, national AI laws (Vietnam), government AI strategies (Singapore, Malaysia), and growing startup activity, SEA is one of the most dynamic AI regions globally.

  5. Geopolitics is shaping AI M&A. China's block of Meta's acquisition of Manus is a warning that high-value AI transactions involving founders or IP with Chinese origins face significant cross-border scrutiny — even when startups are incorporated in neutral jurisdictions like Singapore.

  6. Cybersecurity-fintech convergence is accelerating. Across both India and Southeast Asia, financial institutions are treating cybersecurity as a first-order strategic priority, driving investment in AI-driven fraud detection and quantum-safe infrastructure.
